Hi, I'm Aysha Khan.

I’m a journalist based in Denver. My work focuses on digital culture, religion and underrepresented communities—particularly Muslims in America. That could mean covering an interfaith effort to save net neutrality one week and a new hate crime the next.

These days I work as social media editor at the Religion News Service, where I also report on Muslim issues. Before that, I worked with ThinkProgress, American Journalism Review, the Journalism Center on Children and Families, and The Tempest, where I spent nearly two years editing culture stories and launching the tech vertical.

Get my newsletter ¬

"Creeping Sharia" is a newsletter for and about Muslims in America. You should sign up for it. Hooray for more things in your inbox!

Sent out twice a month, each edition contains a roundup of the best links, with an emphasis on original reporting and providing multiple perspectives on issues. We going to go beyond a list of the latest hate crimes to instead offer you some food for thought. I also feature interesting scholars and resources to learn from.
