Hi, I'm Aysha Khan.

I’m a journalist in Boston. My work examines religion, digital culture and minority communities — with a main focus on the lives of Muslims in America.

I report on U.S. Muslims and millennial spirituality at Religion News Service, a non-profit news wire partnered with the Associated Press. I've filed stories from London to Las Vegas, covering federal anti-extremism programs, Muslim women confronting spiritual abuse and a Uighur woman's battle to release her father from Chinese imprisonment.

I've held fellowships with the GroundTruth Project, the Maynard Institute for Journalism Education, Muslim Women & the Media Training Institute, and Journalism & Women Symposium. My reporting has won awards from the South Asian Journalists Association and the Religion News Association.

Reporting and writing ¬

My reporting has been published in The New York Times, The Washington Post, The Associated Press, USA Today, NBC News, Religion News Service, ThinkProgress, Vice News, American Journalism Review, Religion & Politics magazine, Sojourners, and dozens of other national, regional and local publications.

I'm also available for freelance reporting and editing on projects relating to religion, religious freedom, North American Muslims, digital culture, surveillance and privacy, civic tech and activism, South Asian culture and related issues. I gravitate towards solutions-oriented stories.

Get my newsletter ¬

"Creeping Sharia" is a newsletter by, for and about Muslims in America. Once a month, you'll recieve a comprehensive, in-depth roundup of the latest journalism on Muslims to help you understand the political, cultural, religious and social landscape of American Muslims.

Subscribers include prominent researchers, scholars, journalists, filmmakers, activists and students from some of the world's top educational institutions, news organizations and think tanks. Join us!

For matters related to Religion News Service, you can reach me at my aysha dot b dot khan at religionnews dot com address. (Please note that I am a writer and have no power to publish pieces or approve pitches.) You may add this address to relevant press and media mailing lists. If you would like to send me an advanced copy of your book or film, please also reach out to this email. Do not send any particularly sensitive information to this account.

For secure or particularly sensitive communications, message me via the Signal app if you have my phone number or can request it without drawing attention. Enable disappearing messages if you suspect your phone may be seized or examined. Otherwise, email me at ayshak at protonmail dot com so our messages can be encrypted end-to-end. It's best to send your message from another Protonmail account; you can sign up for free here. Do not include identifying information in your address or subject line. Email me using a device you own personally, and use your home WiFi network or a public network such as at a cafe or library.

Please know that I receive a lot of messages across these and other platforms every day. I'll try my best to respond in a timely fashion but things fall through the cracks sometimes. Feel free to follow up. And if I feel I'm not the right person to cover your story or work with you on a project, I will try to connect you with someone better suited to the task.
